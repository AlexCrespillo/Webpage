About Me

Welcome to my academic portfolio

I am a Predoctoral Researcher (PhD student) at the Institute of Pyrenean Ecology (IPE-CSIC), specializing in hydrology and climate science. My research focuses on understanding and assessing drought phenomena, dry-spell hazards, and developing climate services to support decision-making in water resource management.

With a strong background in environmental sciences and data analytics, I combine advanced statistical methods, machine learning techniques, and geospatial analysis to tackle complex climate-related challenges. My work contributes to building science-informed tools that help communities and policymakers adapt to climate variability and change.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between climate research and practical applications, ensuring that scientific knowledge translates into actionable insights for sustainable water management.

Research & Projects

My doctoral research explores drought dynamics, climate variability, and the development of operational climate services for water resource management in Spain.

PhD Research Focus

Meteorological and Hydrological Droughts Analysis Using AI & Big Data

IPE-CSIC, Zaragoza January 2025 - Present

Investigating the interactions, characteristics, and propagations of meteorological and hydrological droughts and heat waves. Utilizing cutting-edge artificial intelligence techniques and Big Data processing to understand climate extremes and their cascading effects on water resources and ecosystems.

Research Areas

01

Drought Characterization & Assessment

Developing optimal and flexible approaches for drought assessment using multi-scalar indices and advanced statistical methods to capture drought phenomena across different temporal and spatial scales.

02

Spatial Hazard Mapping

Spatial assessment of dry-spell hazards using high-resolution climate data and machine learning to identify vulnerable regions and inform climate adaptation strategies.

03

Operational Climate Services

Creating science-informed maps and operational tools to translate complex climate data into accessible services for stakeholders in agriculture, water management, and policy.

04

Hydrological Modeling

Integrating hydrological models with climate projections to understand future water availability scenarios and support long-term resource planning under changing climate.

PhD Goals & Objectives

My doctoral research aims to advance the understanding of drought dynamics and heat waves through innovative AI-driven methodologies and interdisciplinary approaches.

Doctoral Research Objectives

Advancing Climate Extremes Research through AI and Big Data

01

Understand Drought-Heat Wave Interactions

Investigate the complex interactions and propagation mechanisms between meteorological and hydrological droughts and heat waves, identifying cascading effects and feedback loops that amplify climate extremes in Mediterranean regions.

02

Develop AI-Driven Predictive Models

Create and validate advanced machine learning and deep learning models for drought prediction and characterization, leveraging Big Data processing techniques to handle high-resolution climate datasets and improve forecast accuracy.

03

Characterize Multi-Scale Drought Dynamics

Analyze drought characteristics across different temporal and spatial scales, from local to regional levels, using standardized indices and innovative statistical approaches to capture the full complexity of drought phenomena.

04

Create Operational Climate Services

Develop science-informed tools and climate services that translate research findings into actionable information for water resource managers, policymakers, and stakeholders in agricultural and environmental sectors.
